Only 2 of the top 20 names from 1910-1914 were boys. The two boy names (Wilbur and Orville) were most likely made popular from technological advancements at that time.

Ethel was most likely made popular by Ethel Barrymore a stage actress regarded as "The First Lady of the American Theatre" whose career spanned six decades.
